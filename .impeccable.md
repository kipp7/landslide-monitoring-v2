## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are landslide-monitoring command staff, duty operators, and field-response coordinators using a mobile app during active monitoring and incident escalation. They need to understand terrain risk fast, focus the correct slope segment, and dispatch the next action without digging through dense admin UI.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel spatial, decisive, and engineered. It should evoke control, tension, and trust under pressure rather than generic futurism or decorative dashboards.

### Aesthetic Direction
HarmonyOS-first mobile spatial command interface. Dark by default for control-room and nighttime field use, with a restrained industrial palette built around mineral cyan, hazard ember, and graphite-blue neutrals. The memorable differentiator is a real terrain-driven scene that behaves like the app's operational surface, not a background illustration behind stacked cards.

### Design Principles
1. Let the terrain scene lead; chrome stays on the edge.
2. One dominant moment per screen beats many competing highlights.
3. Information hierarchy comes from spacing, contrast, and motion before extra containers.
4. Interactions should feel like commanding a live model, not browsing a dashboard.
5. Every overlay must justify its visual weight against the 3D scene underneath.
